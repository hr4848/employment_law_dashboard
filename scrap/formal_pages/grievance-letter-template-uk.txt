Grievance Letter Template Example UK
The ‘Grievance Letter Template Aid’ is only £12 encompassing Employment Law and legal phraseology germane to England, Wales & Scotland.
This Grievance Letter Template Aid encompasses all four of the following topics:
1.Toxic working environment complaint letter template UK i.e.,toxic work environment complaint letter example and samples.
2. Work-Related Stress, and your organisations statutory duties to observe a duty of care for you under the auspices of the HSAWA 1974 & MHSWR 1999;
3. Workplace Bullying and Harassment including the Statutory Acts & Regulations your employer ought to observe and implement under the Equality Act 2010 (amongst others);
4. Discrimination in any form, including disability age, sex, sexual, sexual orientation, gender, race, religion, age etc, as encompassed within the Equality Act 2010.
Why Should You Buy A Grievance Letter Template Aid
?
The ‘Grievance Letter Template Aid’ structures the statements within your grievance letter for you under the aforementioned headings, so all you have to do is fill in the blanks where indicated!
The ‘Grievance Letter Template Aid’ demonstrates to your employer that you have taken the time to research employment laws, and have some comprehension of Employment Law. Thus, your employer will undoubtedly treat the grievance letter you submit with more consideration.
Remember: (1) Don’t give your employer an opportunity to wiggle off the hook! (2) A poorly written grievance letter provides your employer every opportunity to rubbish what you have stated (3) An employer’s best defence in receiving an employees grievance letter is a good offence (4) the HR department aren’t your friends – they’re only there to mitigate your employer’s liability!
The ‘Grievance Letter Template Aid’ demonstrates to your employer that you are articulate, and quite capable of pursuing an Employment Tribunal claim, should the need arise.
In addition, the ‘Grievance Letter Template’ includes Eight Steps you ought to undertake before lodging a written formal grievance letter. This section pertains to: (i) acquiring witness statements from workplace colleagues (ii) consulting your GP and what to say (iii) why you should acquire and review your employer’s policies and procedures prior to lodging a grievance letter (iv) workplace risk assessments (v) how to gather evidence to place the burden of proof on your employer etc,.
The Grievance Letter Template Aid also includes Fourteen Things that you ought to state (and not state) within your formal letter of grievance, viz: don’t write anything libelous within your grievance letter which could amount to defamation; establishing your employer’s vicarious liability for harassment, why your employer should observe EHRC guidelines and recommendations for you, your employer’s duty to make reasonable adjustments for you under the Equality Act, fiduciary duties of Company directors to provide you a safe place and system of work, etc,.
The Grievance Letter Template Aid is comprehensive. It gives YOU a fighting chance against both your employer, and their solicitors!
